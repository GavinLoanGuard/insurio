The Problem

What bank mortgage insurance gets wrong

Two structural flaws that affect your clients — and that most brokers never explain.

Coverage shrinks. The premium doesn't.

Bank mortgage insurance covers the outstanding balance — which decreases every year as the mortgage pays down. But the premium your client pays stays the same. They're buying less coverage for the same money, every single month.

Underwriting happens at claim time, not application.

Banks often use post-claim underwriting — they collect premiums for years, then investigate your client's health history only when a claim is filed. Clients can be denied at the exact moment they need the coverage most.

Who to Refer

The clients who need this most

You don't need to refer everyone. These are the borrowers who are most underserved by the bank's group policy.

Self-employed borrowers

Bank group policies often exclude or heavily restrict the self-employed. Individual coverage underwrites them properly and doesn't penalize irregular income.

Clients declined by the bank

Bank policies are one-size-fits-all. We shop 10+ carriers to find coverage for clients who don't fit the standard mold.

Anyone who wants portable coverage

Bank mortgage insurance is tied to the lender. If your client switches lenders or refinances, they lose coverage and start over. Individual policies follow the person, not the mortgage.
